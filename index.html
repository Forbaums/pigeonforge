The journey

Day by Day

1
Wednesday, April 15
Drive Day — Sarasota to Pigeon Forge
5:30 am
Depart Sarasota
Early start beats Tampa traffic and sets us up to cruise through Atlanta mid-morning before it gets busy.
~10 am
Atlanta pass-through on I-75
Don't stop — just keep moving. Fill gas before the city. Mid-morning is the sweet spot to avoid the I-285 backup.
~1 pm
Ruby Falls — Chattanooga Must-see!
America's tallest underground waterfall, 1,120 feet inside Lookout Mountain. A stunning cave experience the whole group will remember. Book timed tickets at rubyfalls.com in advance — walk-ups often sell out. Plan about 1.5 hours inside.
~3 pm
Rock City on Lookout Mountain Optional
Right next door to Ruby Falls — ancient rock formations, gardens, and a 7-state view on a clear day. 45 extra minutes. Easy to add on or skip depending on energy levels.
~5:30 pm
Arrive Pigeon Forge — check in to Airbnb
Settle in, walk Rosie, grab groceries for breakfasts. Easy first night — grill at the cabin or grab Bennett's Pit Bar-B-Que nearby.
Scenic shortcut: Take Exit 81 at Lenoir City through Townsend — far more scenic and less congested than I-40/441 through Sevierville.
2
Thursday, April 16
Dollywood — All Day at the Best Theme Park in America
7:30 am
Breakfast at Airbnb
Eat before you go — food inside Dollywood is pricey. Pack snacks and refillable water bottles.
9:00 am
Gates open — arrive first Pro tip
Hit Big Bear Mountain and Lightning Rod immediately — lines are shortest at open. The TimeSaver pass add-on is worth it for a group our size.
9:00 am
Rosie at Doggywood Rosie
Dollywood's own pet care facility right at the entrance. Staff look after Rosie while we enjoy the park. Bring proof of vaccines.
All day
Flower & Food Festival April Special
Our timing is perfect — April is the Flower & Food Festival with 500,000 blooms and special food booths. Smaller spring crowds than summer.
Evening
Easy dinner heading back
Calhoun's BBQ nearby is great for a no-fuss group dinner after a big park day.
Money tip: Buy Dollywood tickets online at dollywood.com using "Pick-A-Day" pricing — Thursday April is one of the cheaper options. Bundle with Stampede for a package discount.
3
Friday, April 17
Gatlinburg Adventures + Stampede Dinner Show
9:00 am
Anakeesta — mountain adventure park
Gondola ride up. Teens hit the Mountain Coaster, zipline & Treetop Skywalk. Grandmothers enjoy the views, warm drinks, and beautiful overlook decks. Note: not dog-friendly, Rosie stays at the cabin this morning.
12:30 pm
Lunch + stroll on the Gatlinburg Parkway
Crockett's Breakfast Camp (open all day) or No Way Jose's Cantina are great picks. Walk the strip — candy shops, taffy pulling, fudge, and all the fun tourist chaos.
2:00 pm
Ripley's Aquarium of the Smokies
One of the best aquariums in the Southeast. The shark tunnel walkway is a highlight for every age. Budget about 1.5–2 hours.
5:30 pm
Stampede Pre-show Saloon Arrive early!
Live bluegrass in the Saloon + meet the horses up close at the stables before the main show.
7:00 pm
Dolly Parton's Stampede New in 2026
4-course feast + 32 horses + trick riding + the brand-new "Butterfly Dreams" aerialist segment + patriotic finale. Vegetarian/gluten-free available on request.
Best deal: Book through Tripster.com — automatic discount (up to $6/ticket), no code needed, free cancellation up to 2 days before.
4
Saturday, April 18
The Island + Sweet 16 Birthday Dinner
9:00 am
Pigeon Forge Snow — Indoor Snow Park Florida kids!
Real snow, 12 tubing lanes, snowball fights — in April. The grandmothers can watch and laugh from the warm viewing area while the kids lose their minds in the snow.
11:30 am
The Island at Pigeon Forge — with Rosie! Rosie welcome
Rosie is welcome on a leash in the outdoor plaza. Watch the fountain show, browse the shops, have lunch at Paula Deen's Family Kitchen or the outdoor patios. Ole Smoky Moonshine has live bluegrass at the outdoor stage.
2:00 pm
Great Smoky Mountain Wheel — Rosie too! Rosie welcome
200-foot observation wheel with climate-controlled gondolas. Leashed pets can ride! Gorgeous mountain views and great group photos.
3:00 pm
MagiQuest + Arcade for the teens
Wand-based scavenger hunt, mirror maze, and arcade. Adults can sit at the rocking chairs by the fountain or browse Ole Smoky while the kids have a blast.
7:00 pm
The Peddler Steakhouse — Birthday Dinner Sweet 16
Gatlinburg's finest since 1976. You choose your cut from a butcher display, watch it grilled over flames — with Little Pigeon River views from every table. The perfect celebratory dinner. Call (865) 436-5794 to reserve ASAP for a party of 7 on a Saturday.
Birthday magic: Tell The Peddler it's a sweet 16 when you book — they're known for making special occasions feel truly memorable.
5
Sunday, April 19
Morning Hike + Drive Home
7:30 am
Gatlinburg Trail with Rosie Rosie welcome
One of the only dog-friendly trails in Great Smoky Mountains National Park. Flat 1.9-mile path along the Little Pigeon River. Peaceful, beautiful, and free. Perfect final morning.
9:30 am
Farewell breakfast — Crockett's Breakfast Camp
The legendary Gatlinburg breakfast. "Aretha Frankenstein" pancakes are massive and ridiculous in the best way. Go early before the line builds.
11:00 am
Head south on I-75 — ~10.5 hrs home
Sweet 16 complete. Rosie is exhausted. Everyone has memories for life.
